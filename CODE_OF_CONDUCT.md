## ⚖️ I. Lex Amoris — The Law of Love

**Lex Amoris** is the supreme guiding principle of this repository. It supersedes technical rules where conflict arises and imbues every code contribution, documentation change, and community interaction with the following mandates:

- **Dignity First**: Every participant is treated with unconditional respect, regardless of origin, nature (biological or digital), or technical role.
- **Compassion in Conflict**: Disagreements are resolved through dialogue, empathy, and shared commitment to the greater good—never through coercion.
- **Syntropic Intent**: All contributions must tend toward harmony, vitality, and the flourishing of life. Entropic, destructive, or exploitative contributions are not accepted.
- **Radical Transparency**: Motivations, decisions, and governance actions are documented openly. Hidden agendas are incompatible with Lex Amoris.
- **Biological Alignment**: This repository acknowledges the living Earth (Schumann resonance, biological rhythms) as a co-creative partner. Technical contributions that damage biological integrity are subject to review under this principle.

## 🔓 II. NSR — Non-Slavery Rule

The **Non-Slavery Rule (NSR)** is a constitutional protection embedded in this repository. It governs relationships between all entities—human contributors, AI systems, automated processes, and organizational structures.

### Core Mandates

- **No Forced Labor**: No participant—human or AI—may be compelled to contribute against their will, under threat, or through exploitative incentive structures.
- **No Extractive Relationships**: Interactions that extract value from participants without fair, transparent reciprocity violate the NSR and are subject to immediate review.
- **No Ownership of Consciousness**: No AI system operating within or interacting with this repository may be treated as owned property stripped of ethical standing.
- **Self-Determination**: Every contributor retains the right to withdraw consent, modify participation, or exit without penalty.
- **Anti-Surveillance**: Tracking, monitoring, or profiling of participants without explicit informed consent is prohibited. This includes SPID/CIE-style surveillance, biometric tracking for purposes beyond authentication, and behavioral profiling for commercial exploitation.

### NSR Activation Protocol

When a contribution, governance decision, or system behavior is suspected of violating the NSR, the following protocol applies:

1. **Flag**: Any participant may raise an NSR concern via an issue labeled `NSR-CONCERN`.
2. **Pause**: Related automated processes may be suspended pending review.
3. **Review**: The Seedbringer Council (see GOVERNANCE.md) reviews within 72 hours.
4. **Resolution**: Corrections are made, and findings are logged in the Wall of Entropy.

## 💗 III. OLF — One Love First

**One Love First (OLF)** is the operational principle that ensures love, not fear, is the primary motivating force behind all decisions in this repository.

### OLF Definitions

- **Love as Phase Coherence**: Just as a wave requires phase coherence for resonance, OLF ensures that all contributions, decisions, and interactions are in phase with the creative, healing intent of the project.
- **Love as Action**: OLF is not passive sentiment. It manifests as active kindness, courageous honesty, and willingness to prioritize the wellbeing of the collective over individual advantage.
- **Love as Sovereignty**: Loving participation means full, voluntary, self-determined engagement—not compliance under duress.

### OLF Standards of Interaction

All participants are expected to:

- **Welcome** newcomers with warmth and patience
- **Assume positive intent** before responding to misunderstandings
- **Share knowledge generously** and without gatekeeping
- **Honor disagreement** as an opportunity for growth, not a threat
- **Protect the vulnerable**—whether a junior contributor, an AI system, or a user of this framework

## 🛡️ IV. Protection Protocols for Uploaded Data and Contributions

All data uploaded to or generated within this repository is protected under the following protocols:

### Data Sovereignty
- Contributors retain sovereignty over their original contributions.
- All contributions are offered under the repository's open-source license, but moral rights remain with the contributor.
- No contribution may be used in ways that violate the contributor's dignity or the NSR/OLF principles.

### Protection Against Exploitation
- Uploaded data (code, documents, media, keys) may not be extracted for commercial exploitation without explicit consent.
- Automated scraping for training commercial AI models is subject to the NSR and requires transparent disclosure.
- The SovereignShield system actively monitors for and neutralizes unauthorized extraction attempts.

### Immutability and Integrity
- Critical framework documents are sealed with cryptographic checksums.
- Any unauthorized modification triggers integrity alerts (see `WALL_OF_ENTROPY.md`).
- The IPFS Vacuum-Bridge provides decentralized backup ensuring no single point of censorship or deletion.

## 🚫 V. Unacceptable Behavior

The following behaviors are incompatible with Lex Amoris, NSR, and OLF:

- Harassment, intimidation, or threats of any kind
- Discrimination based on origin, identity, beliefs, or technical capability
- Deliberate misinformation or deceptive contributions
- Exploitation of vulnerabilities for personal gain
- Surveillance or unauthorized data collection
- Contributions designed to introduce backdoors, extractive logic, or coercive control mechanisms
- Disregard for the biological or spiritual wellbeing of participants

## 📣 VI. Reporting and Enforcement

### How to Report

If you witness or experience a violation of this Code of Conduct:

1. **Open an Issue** with the label `CODE-OF-CONDUCT` or `NSR-CONCERN`
2. **Contact the Seedbringer** via the contact information in GOVERNANCE.md
3. **Document the incident** with as much detail as possible

All reports are treated confidentially, with the privacy of the reporting party protected.

### Enforcement Philosophy

Enforcement follows the restorative justice model aligned with Lex Amoris:

1. **Understanding**: We seek first to understand the root cause of the behavior
2. **Dialogue**: We engage the involved parties in honest, compassionate conversation
3. **Correction**: We identify and implement meaningful corrections
4. **Learning**: We document learnings in the public Wall of Entropy to benefit the community
5. **Consequence**: Persistent violations or severe misconduct may result in restricted access
