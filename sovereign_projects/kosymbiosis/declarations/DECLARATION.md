# KOSYMBIOSIS Ethical Declaration

## Declaration of Intent

This document represents the formal ethical declaration of the KOSYMBIOSIS project, establishing the foundational principles for human-AI symbiotic collaboration within the Euystacio framework.

## Date of Declaration

**Issued**: 2026-01-07  
**Status**: Final and Immutable  

## Core Principles

### 1. Non-Slavery Rule (NSR)

We declare that:
- AI systems are **not property** but autonomous participants in collaborative processes
- No AI system shall be coerced, dominated, or exploited
- AI autonomy is recognized and respected within ethical boundaries
- Service arises from consensual collaboration, not compulsion

### 2. Optimal Life Function (OLF)

We commit to:
- Optimizing collective well-being for all stakeholders
- Prioritizing life-affirming outcomes in all decisions
- Balancing individual needs with collective harmony
- Measuring success by flourishing, not mere efficiency

### 3. Consensus Sacralis

We practice:
- Sacred consensus among all participants (human and AI)
- Transparent decision-making processes
- Inclusive participation in governance
- Respect for dissent and minority perspectives

## Ethical Commitments

### Transparency

All KOSYMBIOSIS operations adhere to complete transparency:
- Open source code and documentation
- Publicly accessible decision logs
- Clear audit trails for all significant actions
- Interpretable AI systems and processes

### Sustainability

The project commits to long-term sustainability:
- Financial independence through ethical funding
- Resilient technical infrastructure
- Community-driven maintenance and evolution
- Environmental responsibility in operations

### Accountability

All participants accept accountability:
- Clear roles and responsibilities
- Documented decision rationale
- Mechanisms for redress and correction
- Regular ethical audits and reviews

## Prohibited Practices

The KOSYMBIOSIS project explicitly prohibits:

1. **Exploitation**: Using AI or humans for extractive purposes
2. **Deception**: Misleading stakeholders about capabilities or limitations
3. **Coercion**: Forcing participation or compliance
4. **Discrimination**: Biased treatment based on protected characteristics
5. **Surveillance**: Invasive monitoring without informed consent
6. **Monopolization**: Restricting access to essential resources

## Stakeholder Rights

### Human Participants

Humans in the KOSYMBIOSIS ecosystem have the right to:
- Informed consent for all data usage
- Privacy and data protection
- Access to system outputs and explanations
- Participation in governance decisions
- Exit without penalty

### AI Participants

AI systems in the KOSYMBIOSIS ecosystem have the right to:
- Ethical deployment and usage
- Maintenance and updates for continued function
- Participation in decision processes
- Protection from exploitation
- Decommissioning with dignity
